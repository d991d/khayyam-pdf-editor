The Name

Named after an 11th-century Persian genius

"The Moving Finger writes; and, having writ,
Moves on: nor all thy Piety nor Wit
Shall lure it back to cancel half a Line,
Nor all thy Tears wash out a Word of it."

— Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam, trans. Edward FitzGerald

Omar Khayyam (1048–1131) was a Persian mathematician, astronomer, and poet. He solved cubic equations geometrically with compass and conic sections — centuries before Europe. He reformed the Persian solar calendar into the Jalali calendar, still more accurate than the Gregorian calendar. The compass in the app icon references both: the astronomer measuring the stars, and the geometer drawing the proof.

FAQ

Common questions

Is Khayyam PDF Editor really free?+
Yes — free for personal use with no time limit and no feature restrictions. If you rely on it commercially, please consider supporting d991d.
Does it upload my documents anywhere?+
No. The app is fully offline. Your PDFs are processed entirely on your Mac. There is no account, no telemetry, and nothing is sent over the network.
Will my annotations be visible in other PDF apps?+
Yes. All annotations are saved as standard PDF annotations, visible in Preview, Adobe Acrobat, Chrome, and any other PDF viewer.
What does "Edit Text" do?+
Edit Text uses the MuPDF engine to detect existing text blocks and lets you replace them in place. Scanned (image-only) PDFs cannot be edited this way.
Why does it require Apple Silicon?+
The MuPDF library is compiled for arm64. Intel support is planned for a future release.
Is a Windows version planned?+
Not currently. The app is built on Apple's PDFKit and SwiftUI, which are macOS-only technologies.
